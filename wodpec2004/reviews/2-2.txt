>4) JUSTIFICATION AND GENERAL COMMENTS:


RM-ODP does not provide a vocabulary and grammar for each viewpoint (p.2). 
Rather, it provides a system of concepts and structuring rules that demonstrate 
the relationships between concepts. Clause 7 (Part 2) of RM-ODP provides only 
a very general linguistic framework.


It is essential to observe that viewpoint concepts and structuring rules are 
based on fundamentals described in Part 2 of RM-ODP, and therefore usage of 
these Part 2 concepts ought to be made explicit in any (kind of) modeling.


The semantics of a model is defined not only by "the vocabulary and rules of 
the computational language" (page 4) because fundamental concepts and rules 
(of Part 2) are essential for specifying and understanding of this semantics.


The authors' observations that "During platform-independent modelling, a 
pre-defined abstract platform model may be composed with the model of the 
distributed application" is very important and deserve more detailed discussion, 
especially taking into account the RM-ODP definition of a composition explicitly 
based on property determination. See also [1,2].
