WODPEC 2004 -- Agenda (DRAFT)

This first section is for the workshop organizers' eyes only. It will disappear as soon as we agree on the agenda and general approach for organizing the workshop.

The workshop may be organized around four main parts. 

  • Part 1: Introduction. 
  • Part 2: RM-ODP basics
  • Part 3: Tools and Applications
  • Part 4: Workshop conclusions

The Introduction part will present the schedule, contents and objective of the workshop, and will also serve to introduce the participants of the workshop. (20' aprox.)

The "RM-ODP basics" part will cover the (5) papers that address general issues of the RM-ODP. The "Tools and Applications" part will cover the (5) papers that deal with applications of the RM-ODP, tool support for it, and its relationship with MDA. (The papers that will go in each part are detailed below.)

These two main parts will share a common structure: the papers presentation (20' each), followed by a wrap-up discussion (30'-40' aprox). The items to be identified during the presentation of the papers and the wrapping up session at the end of each part could be the following:

  • Issues, limitations, and current problems of RM-ODP
    • ...
  • Requirements for modifications, enhancements, and developments of RM-ODP
    • ...
  • Issues for the Study Group
    • ...
  • Identification of future work
    • e.g., tools, etc.

The last part ("Conclusions") will try to indentify the conclusions of the workshops. The idea is that this part not only concludes the workshop, but also is used to write down the final issues that the Study Group may undertake, and to outline future works, useful tools that may be developed, etc. It is important WODPEC participants can take something with them after the workshop: agreed ideas, work-to-do, ... (30'-40' aprox.)

Regarding session chairing, Peter could chair Part 2, Bryan Part 3, and I could chair Parts 1 and 4.

Since the EDOC workshops are scheduled into four sessions, we need to split our four parts to accommodate to these sessions. The workshop schedule presented at the end of this page tries to achieve that, although I do not like the results very much (especially, that there is a session with just one paper, which seems to be relegated from the rest, i.e., an "orphan" paper :-) Any ideas on what can be done with this?

There is also a small section dedicated at providing some indications to the papers' presenters. Here it is shown right at the beginning of the page, although it may well even go at the end (i.e., first the workshop agenda and then the recommendations). The first option is good since it forces all visitors of this page (which are basically the paper presenters) to go through the recommendations first, and then read the agenda. However, it is not so elegant as the second option. Which one do you prefer? 

Finally, please feel free to add/remove/change anything you may dislike or think that can be improved in this page. Thank you.

General recommendations for presenters

  • Each paper has been allocated a 20 minutes slot: 15' for presentation, and 5' for questions. Please do bear in mind that you only have 15' for presenting your paper when preparing your presentation (which roughly means 15 slides). Session chairs will be very strict with the duration of the presentation.
  • There will be a PC and a beamer at the workshop for you to present your paper. You could either take your personal computer with you and connect it to the beamer, or produce a PowerPoint or PDF version of your presentation to be displayed from the PC at the workshop. Please use either a CD or a USB flash memory stick to transfer the file with your presentation to that PC. Floopy disks or other kinds of media are discouraged since the PC there may not be able to read them.
  • During the workshop we will try to identify some issues that need to be addressed by the RM-ODP revision Study Group, or by future development or research. Such issues include:
    • Current issues, limitations, and problems of the RM-ODP
    • Requirements for modifications, enhancements, and developments of RM-ODP
    • Identification of future work (e.g. tools, etc.)

    Please think about these issues in advance, and try to bring them forward during the presentation of your paper or during the workshop discussions.
