- Weak points of the paper:
  The only weak point I see is the fact that on the case study we can see objects related to Loan, Fine, etc., but it is not clear if we are speaking of the computational objects corresponding to the related information objects or the information objects themselves. It would have been interesting to include some comments on how this viewpoint is related to the information viewpoint and show some correspondences.   

- Strong points of the paper:
  It proposes a UML2 representation of ODP Conceptual viewpoint concepts and it illustrate them through an easy to understand case study. I think the use of a common case study to illustrate those mappings is really useful.
  
- Any suggestions for improvements: 
  1) Clarity about how information objects are related to computational objects.
  2) On Figure 5, I think what is draw as an "alternate" is in fact an "optional", as even if the condition is true you will be proceeding with the steps 6 and 7. So you must replace "alt" by "opt".
