- Summary and main contributions:


The paper gives an overview of current architectural approaches - Service Oriented Architectures (SOA), Model Driven Architectures (MDA), Component Based Architecture (CBA) and Event Driven Architectures (EDA) to Service Oriented Architectures, Identity Enabled Networks and Utility Compute Architectures (UCA) - and discusses their interrelationships and value propositions. It provides a more detailed discussion of the RM-ODP (although some parts of this are just direct quotations from the standard). It highlights the significance of the architectural approaches to RM-ODP and maps the key concepts behind each of the architectural approaches to the RM-ODP.


- Weak points of the paper:


The key purpose of the paper namely discussion of "the significance of these architectural approaches to RM-ODP and maps the key concepts behind each of these architectural approaches to the RM-ODP" is somewhat cursory and, in particular, does not explain the practical benefit to be derived from bringing together the architectural approaches and the ODP concepts.


The paper gives the impression of being assembled in a hurry and has not been carefully proofread. There are no references for work cited.


- Strong points of the paper:


There are suggestions that part of the background to the paper has involved the practical application of RM-ODP concepts in a project (including the use of UML as a notation) - but this is not elaborated.


- Any suggestions for improvements:


The paper could be significantly improved by restructuring so that the discussion of the RM-ODP came first (with rather more information on its application in the IntelliGrid project), followed by the overview of architectural approaches merged with the discussion of the significance of the architectural approaches to the RM-ODP and the mapping of concepts.
