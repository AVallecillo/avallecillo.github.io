SOME GENERAL COMMENTS ABOUT THE REVIEWS

Usually, papers submitted to a workshop present initial ideas and works, 
which are not necessarily very mature yet, and most authors are seeking 
some feedback from the reviewers (who are supposed to be the "experts" 
on the topics of the workshop). This feedback can be in any form: just 
providing comments about the work (its feasibility, relevance, 
originality,...); indications and links to other related works, 
projects and experiences that the authors are not aware of; etc. 

Basically, the authors want to know whether their work may be relevant 
to the community/industry interested in these topics, whether it is worth 
continuing (starting :-) working on the project presented in the paper, 
and some hints and guidance on how to do it properly.

Other kind of papers just try to raise issues for discussion at the workshop. 
In this case, the reviewer can help the authors know of other people/projects 
also related to these issues, whether the issues are really relevant (or the 
issues have been solved long time ago :-), and even give them indications to 
more issues which are related to those raised in the paper.

Your acceptance/rejection decision should consider whether the submitted work: 

- is adequate to the workshop scope and list of topics;
- seems to be feasible; 
- presents something interesting to the potential participants and readers of the
  workshop proceedings;
- may foster interesting discussions during the workshop.
