- Summary and main contributions:

	The authors discuss the representation of Engineering Viewpoint and
	Technology Viewpoint specifications in UML 2.0.

	I suggest the paper be positioned as exploring the issues in the relation 
	between UML 2.0 and the Engineering and Technology Viewpoints, and not as 
	proposing solutions for this representation, since the work is still rather 
	immature. This should be reflected in the paper's title, introduction and 
	conclusions for it to be accepted.


- Weak points of the paper:

	The issues are discussed briefly. Further insight would improve the discussion.

	The presentation should be improved (language and structure of the paper). 

	The examples should be better detailed. Currently, it is hard to assess the proposal and its implications.

	Relation to other work is not discussed. In particular, what is the relation 
	with the current draft of ISO/IEC 19793, ITU-T X.906 "Use of UML for specification 
	of ODP systems" [http://www.lcc.uma.es/~av/UML4ODP/MAL-019%20v01-03.PDF] ?
	What is the relation of this work and the metamodel for the Engineering Viewpoint 
	provided in that draft?



- Strong points of the paper:

	The topic is relevant, and is a current issue in standardization activities.


- Any suggestions for improvements:

	I would suggest to focus on some of the issues and provide more details. 

	I am surprised by the suggestion to use Enterprise Viewpoint concepts in 
	the Engineering Viewpoint (section 2.4). I see no arguments or evidence to 
	suggest this is a good idea. The same applies to the representation of 
	"Implementation" in the Technology Viewpoint as a UML Activity Diagram.

	The correspondence between the Engineering Viewpoint and the Computational 
	Viewpoint defined in the paper excludes replication, since there is a 
	one-to-one relationship between Computation Objects and Basic Engineering Objects.
	(see 10.2 in RM-ODP Part 3)
	"Except where transparencies which replicate objects are involved, each 
	computational interface corresponds exactly to one engineering interface, 
	and that engineering interface corresponds only to that computational interface."

	Some Figures from RM-ODP referred to should be included in the paper. Is there 
	any relation between these Figures and the examples in UML?

	The examples should be better explained. In particular, the figures should be 
	described in the text. Is there any relation between Figure 3 and 4? 



5) FURTHER COMMENTS FOR AUTHORS:

	References should be added in the introduction. Caption of Figure 5 missing.

	I do not understand the sentence "We believe we have choices e.g. on 
	abstract vs. instance..." (conclusions). What do you mean with "abstract vs. instance"?
