4) JUSTIFICATION AND GENERAL COMMENTS:

The paper presents a technique for deriving a MOF-based metamodel
from a DTD representing the syntax of a web conceptual model. The
contribution is rather limited from the technical standpoint, but
goes in the right direction of mapping proprietary web modeling
notations into a shared format that will allow formal comparison and
possibly some unification of the MDWE methodologies.


 - Weak points of the paper:

The transformation of an XML DTD is quite a simple task and there
are antecedents of DTD processing, cited by the authors.

The authors have implemented their method, but do nor provide a
description of the tool.

There are already some other methodologies equipped with MOF
metamodels; the authors could have attempted a first-cut comparison
of features.

Mapping a domain specific language like WebML into UML using a meta
model keeps the comparison at the syntactic level and does not
attain the semantic level. Instead, using a UML profile would force
the correlation of domain specific language features to the standard
semantics of UML concepts. This is a complementary, but very useful,
way of investigating existing design notations, which tries to find
a common denominator also at the semantic level.

 - Strong points of the paper:

The problem of finding a common denominator to the variety of web
modeling notations proposed in the web engineering community is a
relevant and the paper provides a useful step in this direction.



The method for deriving the MOF-based metamodel is systematic, so in
principle it could be applied to other DTD-baed notations.

The paper is well written and clear.

The mapping to Ecore could provide a basis for implementing an
editor of WebML diagrams in  the Eclipse framework and for building
useful tools like multi-language model-checker and code generators
in that feature-rich and popular environment, contributing to the
widespread adoption of MDWE methods.

The availability of an EMF-based tool could provide a basis for
comparing MOF-based and ad-hoc tool implementation. Metamodel-based
tools are more flexible and interoperable, but their performance in
editing and generating code with real-world large specifications
needs to be assessed.
