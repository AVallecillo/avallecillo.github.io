4) JUSTIFICATION AND GENERAL COMMENTS:

The paper deals with the semi-automatic translation of UML component
& connector view diagrams into SO-SAM, a formal model based on
algebraic Petri Nets and temporal logic. The motivation is the need
of a more formal representation of architectures for a better
analysis of properties. However, the paper does not contain any
example of such analysis. Besides, the addressed systems have very
little to do with the web, as they represent generic
component-based, message-driven  architectures.

- Summary and main contributions:

The paper presents an approach for translating UML component and
connector view diagrams (a kind of class diagrams), augmented with
statecharts for message protocol disambiguation, into So-SAM
specifications, which are based on Petri nets, algebraic
specifications  and temporal logic. The ultimate goal should be to
enable a deeper analysis of system properties.

- Weak points of the paper:

The paper has several weak points:

- the sound and completeness of the translation is not demonstrated,
but only claimed informally

- the promised deeper level of analysis is not demonstrated. The
claim is that the SO-SAM model enables deeper reasoning because it
exposes hidden semantics embedded implicitly in the UML class
diagrams and statecharts. But there is no example of the usage of
such explicit information exploited for assessing useful system
properties. In absence of such result, the translation is little
more than an exercise, which represents the system architecture with
a more elaborate (and difficult to master) approach.

- the technical writing is rather poor. Some temporal formulas are
ill defined and not clear. For instance the "circle" operator is
used at some point, but is does not seem to be defined.

- the authors say that it is impossible to prove the correctness of
the transformation. Why is this so? Statecharts have a formal
semantics, class diagrams have a formal syntactic structure, and the
bindings between the two could be formalized as well. So it should
be possible to prove the correctness of the transformation.

The idea of using extended Petri Nets for validation is not new.
What is the original contribution of SO-SAM? How does it take into
account specific features of web systems? What are the benefits of
using temporal logic instead of simpler logical languages capable of
describing the semantics of events and actions  (e.g., transaction
logic - http://www.cs.toronto.edu/~bonner/transaction-logic.html).
You do not use "time" after all.

- Strong points of the paper:

I understand the goal of formalizing UML specifications in order to
allow deeper reasoning. But I would be more convinced by some
examples of the benefits of resorting to a more complex formal
framework like SO-SAM.


 - Any suggestions for improvements:

Use the transformation to prove non obvious properties of the
architecture.

Relate your work to the literature on system verification based on
Petri Nets and to the work on the formalization of UML.

Make a better connection to **Web** applications and architectures.


Revise the English writing.

Check for grammar errors and cut sentences, like this, on page 1:

"The importance of web system architecture descriptions has been
widely.. "

Remove this item from the footer in page 1: IWSOSE’06, May
2728,2006, ShangHai, China

Check the names of model elements in the figures and in the running
text, because sometimes they do not match, like at page 5 - Rule 2 -
second bullet, where you use RawData instead of RawDataInput,
frameout instead of FrameOutput, and dataready, which is not in
figure 2 (a).
