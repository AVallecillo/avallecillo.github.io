- Summary and main contributions:
The paper presents a pattern-based software development process.
The main peculiarity is that the adopted design patterns are achieved
by the integration of software engineering patterns and HCI patterns.
This allows one to take into account non-functional qualities,
such as usability, generally neglected in the SE field.

- Weak points of the paper:
The presented approach seems quite general for software systems.I am
wondering if the authors have worked on integrating also proper Web Engineering issues.
Several Web Engineering patterns have been so far proposed (see for example
papers by Rossi&Schwabe published in 2000).

- The adoption of the presented method is quite unfeasible without a tool's support.
Indeed, in the conclusions authors say that they are working on the implementation of
a tool complementing their method. It would be interesting to anticipate
some details about how a tool can make the PF selection and the different transformations automatic.
In which measure can the planned tool support any planned transformation step? Is there any limitation?

- Strong points of the paper:
The paper is well written, and the methodology is well presented: it adequately
describes all the step required to transform problem frames into problem
solutions, and to integrate patterns from SE and HCI.

- Any suggestions for improvements:
I would suggest authors to clarify the two points I mentioned above.
English must be improved.
