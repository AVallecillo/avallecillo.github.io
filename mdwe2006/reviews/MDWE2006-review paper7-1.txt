You may also include    general comments, relevant for both author(s)    and the Programme Committee.) - Summary and main contributions:
The authors propose to add an addition model, the operation model, to the models commonly used in Web Design methods, being the content model, the composition/navigational  model and the presentation model. The model would be used to capture the operations that can be called in a web page to manipulate the content. From the description given in the paper, this model look similar to the operations that can be defined for classes in UML.    - Weak points of the paper:
Paper is not very convincing. It is not clear if the approach indeed overcomes the limitations of other approaches. 
The operational model as presented seems to be a list of available operations for each class. How are these operations specified?
It is also not clear how to deal with complex operations that deals with different classes and relationships. The graphical notation is far too limited to be able to handle this. 
The authors say that only WebML and W2000 are web design methods that allow to specify operations. This is not true.- Strong points of the paper:
- Any suggestions for improvements: Give examples of more advanced operations.
Explain the difference with OO modeling where the operations are included in the classes. Why is your approach better?
