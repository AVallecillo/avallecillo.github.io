4) JUSTIFICATION AND GENERAL COMMENTS:
 (A few lines are mandatory here to justify the numerical evaluation. You may also include general comments, relevant for both author(s) and the Programme Committee.)


In this paper the authors propose a metamodel for WebML. The metamodel is generated by a semi-automatic process that is defined to produce MOF-based metamodels from DTDs. It is an interesting approach. The process is clearly described and technically sound. The use of MOF and UML is correct.  The paper is well written and well structured.



- Summary and main contributions:
The main contributions of this paper is on the one hand the resulting MOF-based metamodel for the Web modeling language WebML. On the other hand, the paper presents the process used for the generation of this metamodel. The Metamodel Generator, the transformation rules and the heuristics used from the conversion of the WebML DTD to the MOF-based metamodel are described with enough details. In addition, the main problems identified during the refactoring process are presented, and solutions are proposed for these problems.
 
- Weak points of the paper:
------


- Strong points of the paper:
See main contributions. Additionally, the clarity of text and diagrams result in a good motivation for reading the paper and a high understandability.


- Any suggestions for improvements:


- Figure 3 is hard to read. Move <<enumeration>> classes below the other classes in order to allow for enlargement of the figure.
- In Figure 5 rename package Content with Structure or change the text below the Figure.
- Define subpackages for the package Hypertext in order to present a clear overview or at least present two separate views (one including hierarchies and another showing associations) as e.g. the UML Superstructure Document does.
- Quantify the manual effort to produce the WebML metamodel presented in this paper based on the intermediate result produced automatically by the MetaModel Generator  (MMG).
- For the use of aspect-orientation for customization of Web applications refer to Baumeister et al., Modeling Adaptivity with Aspects, in proceedings of ICWE 2005, LNCS 3579.
- Indicate quantifiable efforts of the refactoring process.


5) FURTHER COMMENTS FOR AUTHORS:


Interesting open questions that could be addressed in the paper are: - Validation of the result, i.e. of the WebML metamodel.
- Application of the process to other modeling language specification, such as RDF documents.
