- Summary and main contributions:
The authors describe a project for the development of a Web-based research-lab support system. In the context of the project they discuss several aspects related to Identity and Access Management, organizational structure and its impact on the architecture, and content-management. The W2000 modeling language was used for the project and extended for a specific purpose.


- Weak points of the paper:
The paper lacks structure and real content. 

Your introduction starts with an interesting topic (support system for researcher/labs) ñ but instead of creating a real awareness of the problem, you describe in the middle of this section how you started, what you applied etc.
--> here you should solely focus on the problem ñ nothing else! I want to know after this section, why I should continue reading your paper (i.e. being interested in a solution of the important problem that you should have motivated at this point).

ìThe proposed structureî ñ has nothing to do with research. It might be interesting for the novice reader in the field, but for more advanced readers it sounds simply naive. Knowledge transfer within a group and processes to force innovation are very important topics, but it seems you did not do your homework on this topic (this is why there are no references in this section). There is a lot of work on this topic, you might start reading literature from the biz-gurus in the field, like e.g. Stephen Covey, Peter Drucker, Tom Peters, Gary Hamel to name just a few ñ and then check what has been done in the context of IT (this is what you focus on), like content management, score boards, EAI, SOA, Identity and Access Management (IAM), knowledge management, etc. --> Here I would expect some discussion on State of the art (what other have done so far) ñ and may be some requirements for a solution.

The section 2.1-2.3 are discussing some important aspects of the solution. Unfortunately, the discussed aspects are very superficial. In the context of project structure (2.1) check for more advanced solution e.g. Microsoft approach to development support within visual studio 2005 has some interesting aspects (just to begin with). Section 2.2-2.3 are ridiculous ñ check recent publications on RBAC (role based access control) or even better user/identity management in the context of federated web applications - just to see the difference between your approach and what is already possible today (and was published at the latest WWW and ICWE conferences to name just some prominent places).

Section 3 ñ was the section I thought I could find some interesting aspects, especially about the announced fractal-like approach. The problem here is that you expect all ready to know about W2000. Even though I know it, I am pretty sure that many readers donít ñ and therefore also donít understand what you propose. Is the ìextended aggregateî the whole story about the fractal-like approach. I am disappointed. Another problem related to structure and easy to see is there is a section 3.1 ñ but where is 3.2? From my experience this is the first ìhintî for a reviewer showing that this paper has some problems. --> in this section I would have liked to read about your approach and the solution concepts.

Section 4 & 5 ñ These sections are the only sections that have a ìstructural fitî. Based on the poor concepts the solution architecture is too simple. Why do you need an FTP server for uploading files ñ why not use WebDAV and integrate files and data both in the database, to have consistent data. What do you mean by using the term scalability - how does your approach support scaling out the solution?
