- Summary and main contributions:

The paper discusses an approach and associated architecture 
to make websites accessible in an aural way. The approach 
is illustrated with an example   

- Weak points of the paper:
none

- Strong points of the paper:

Attention is paid to design issues. The authors explicitly deal 
with the fact that it is not sufficient to read a page sequentially. 
The user should have different interaction primitives to "navigate" 
through a website and the aural structure of a webpage may be 
different from a visual page. 

- Any suggestions for improvements: 

Can you indicate how the aural structure is derived? 
Is this still a manual process?
