- Summary and main contributions:

The main innovation with respect to the OOWS is the introduction of further
steps to a development process of web solutions which are related to activities
needed to perform when external functionality is to be integrated into a web
solution.

- Weak points of the paper:

The paper deals just with web applications which ask external services for some
information. It is not clear from the paper how the method is suited for
integration of the whole external transactions with partial user interaction
within the transactions. It seems that authors did not consider this situation.
Workshop discussion in this context could be beneficial for authors and perhaps
also for the community. Also, it is not clear what is a global contribution of
this extension as similar extensions have been published already for example in
the context of WebML (web services and workflows extension to WebML by Marco
Brambrilla et al.), UWE (Business Process Modeling for UWE published at UML
2004),  WIED (information flows in integration with business processes), or UML-
Guide (semantic web apps. where state machines have been used to integrate
external web services into navigation models). Authors should extend the paper
with a comparison to related work.

- Strong points of the paper:

UML/MDA based.

- Any suggestions for improvements:

Authors mention the MDA but it is not clearly described how they benefit from
MDA. Just a short paragraph is devoted to the MDA approach and transformation
itself. This would be a strong part of the paper if it is ready already.
Workshop discussion could be beneficial for the authors in this context as well.
