- Summary and main contributions:
This paper describing the PIM to PSM transformation nicely follows up 
on earlier work by (one of) the authors; the authors might though 
describe the innovation presented in this paper a bit more explicitly.

- Weak points of the paper:

Chapters 3 and 4 provide the user with many details explaining the 
figures, and give the paper a rather "well yeah, I guess that makes sense" 
nature.

The "semi-formal" presentation of the transformation rules in chapter 5 
give the ideas in the paper more a "quasi-formal" appearance.


- Strong points of the paper:

The paper gives a good presentation of the engineering done and this 
is nice for fellow-engineers busy with the same problem. The other side 
of the coin is that outsiders might want to see what this engineering brings 
in terms of the results gained from this effort (there is no discussion on 
a real application). 

- Any suggestions for improvements:

Explain why different PIms for dynamic and advanced navigation are a good idea.

Explain what the consequences are of the choice to program the transformations 
(last sentence in chapter 6).

5) FURTHER COMMENTS FOR AUTHORS:

There are some small typo's like "with with".

The discussion of related work might include more of a comparison with 
related work, and maybe a discussion how research in this area should progress.


6) CONFIDENTIAL COMMENTS FOR PC:
   (not to be sent to authors)

It is certainly relevant and it is described in great detail, but it does not 
include a spectacular innovation. For the solid contribution, participation in 
the workshop is acceptable, but it is likely to be not much reason for 
discussion at the workshop itself.
