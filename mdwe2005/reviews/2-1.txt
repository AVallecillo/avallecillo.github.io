- Summary and main contributions:
The paper proposes a process based on the MDA approach for the 
development of the portlets business logic. This proposal transform 
Entity-Relationships and automaton models into XML descriptions to 
build the final Java implementation.

- Weak points of the paper:
Authors address the generation of the portlet business logic in a very 
simplistic way. Other issues such as how to perform the interaction with 
different Broker Agents would have been more interesting.
No related work is mentioned in the paper.

- Strong points of the paper:
The paper proposes the application of the MDA guidelines for the construction 
of portlets business logic.


- Any suggestions for improvements: 
It is not clear why you depict the automation model (in figure 4) as graphical 
and XML descriptions when you state that they are equivalent.
The name of the entities and relationships included in the ERM presented in fig. 5 
seems not to be quite appropriate. They are supposed to represent the domain, not 
the events. Therefore, I would recommend you to model Clients, Trips and LineTrips 
instead of Users, Requests and Responses.

5) FURTHER COMMENTS FOR AUTHORS:
Regarding the related work, it would be interesting to know if there is any other 
proposal that faces the portlet development process. 


6) CONFIDENTIAL COMMENTS FOR PC:
   (not to be sent to authors)
The topic covered in the work is very interesting, however the proposal presented 
does not provide any novelty idea that most Web Engineering Methods do not use for
Web development. In spite of that, I think that a discussion about the whole process 
of portlet development is really suitable to this workshop.
