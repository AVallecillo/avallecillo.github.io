- Summary and main contributions:

The authors describe a novel approach to deal with the design and development of web apps from a functional point of view. They claim that functional programming languages provide powerful capabilities to execute web design specs as a prototype of a web system. 

- Weak points of the paper:

Although this reviewer does not follow the state-of-the-art in the area of functional programming very closely, he believes that this paper presents an interesting contribution. However, the paper lacks a proper evaluation of the proposed method. For instance, although the case study is well introduced, it only illustrates the method and does not provide an idea of the effort required to use it. Therefore, it would be interesting to include, with a case study discussion, a users' assessment of the method. 

The navigation structure model has important limitations. I didn't see how to specify filters that can be applied when the user follows links. Neither, how the designer can specify which services - that belongs to the business logic- can be exposed to the web user in the navigation. These limitations have been solved by most well-recognized web design methods. 

No methodological support to apply your proposal is provided.  


- Strong points of the paper:

I have no doubt that functional programming can provide a new perspective in the way web applications are specified at the present time. The authors' work opens the door to this challenge.

- Any suggestions for improvements:

You should improve the expressiveness of your navigation diagram including filters and services (functions). 

You should introduce a methodological support for your approach. Starting from the notation, you should describe briefly the set of guidelines for performing the modeling activities in a coherent, consistent, and repeatable manner. In this way, you get as a result a systematic procedure to carry-out the design of a web application from a functional perspective.

You should revise in-depth the most prominent web design methods that have been proved successful in the field of Web Engineering and then compare your work with these methods to demonstrate the strength of your approach.
