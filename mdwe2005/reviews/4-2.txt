This paper is certainly worth to be presented in the workshop. It gives an
excellent description how the travel agency system is modled with OOWS, and
extends OOWS by a services model for the specification of external services. It
is very well written.


With regard to the model-driven approach, the paper describes the different
models used by OOWS and explains them well using the example of the travel
agency system. 


With regard to the transformations between different models, two transformation
are presented. One is the structural transformation between the CIM and the
structural PIM in section 5.1 which is straightforward. The PIM business
process modeling of the purchase offer process, as described in section 5.3,
seems to me to be a refinement of a node from figure 3, and not a model
transformation. Section 5.3 defines then two business processes for two of the
three tasks of the broker agent task taxonomy. However, the broker agent task
is unfortunately not shown in section 4; perhaps it could be added to section 4
since the broker agent is an important example in the paper.


Section 6 introduces a PSM for the services model. Astonishingly, this PSM is
not generated from the PIM, but built manually. The explanation is that the
sergvices PSM describes external providers that may use syntax and semantics
that are different from the PIM. Since that differs from the usual use of PIM
and PSM, the paper should refer to that difference and comment on it. Section 7
shows how a Web service is generated for the broker agent from the business
process model.


In all other areas described by the paper, no PSM is used, but the PIM
transformed directly into code. It might be worthwhile to comment on that
approach, since it differs substantially from the mainly used two, resp.
three-transformation step approach PIM-PSM-code, or CIM-PIM-PSM-code. Could you
explain why no PSM is used?


Overall, the paper is well-written and very interesting; but the model
transformations play a less important role. It would be interesting to see the
transformations that the OlivaNova transformation engine performs.
