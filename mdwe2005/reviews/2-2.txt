- Summary and main contributions:


The authors propose an MDA approach to portlet development based
on ER and state diagrams.


- Weak points of the paper:


The paper lacks a clear problem statement, which makes it difficult 
to asses. The style of writing is somewhat 'flat', i.e., too descriptive, 
with little stress on what is really important or original.


The method proposed considers only the business logic, ignoring GUI 
aspects which, as the authors admit, are an important part of portlets.
The modelling approach is not entirely convincing, considering the 
state of the art.


- Strong points of the paper:


The paper is generally well-written. An example is provided which 
illustrates all aspects and stages of the method, including a mapping 
of the models to the XML descriptors and Java classes that make up the 
portlet.


- Any suggestions for improvements:


ER diagrams are not really first choice for the problem at hand: 
Since the entities you model have behavior, class diagrams would be 
much more suitable. Moreover, it says in p.6, bottom of 1st column, 
that "parameter types of methods have to be inserted manually" because 
they cannot be deduced from the state machine. This is exactly where 
class diagrams would make the difference!


Also, in UML terms, the state transition diagrams depicted specify 
sequences of actions without events. This is much better captured by 
activity diagrams than statecharts.
