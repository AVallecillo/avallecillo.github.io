- Summary and main contributions:

The paper discusses (yet) another approach for Web Application 
Development. The presentation of the ideas does not particularly 
clarify what the significant innovation is, but on the other hand 
it nicely shows what could possibly be an effective approach.

- Weak points of the paper:

There are a large number of models discussed, and the paper 
could be strengthened in its presentation of how the relationships 
between models are found and managed, and thus how the integrated 
design is obtained. The paper could mention more related approaches 
(and make a comparison).

 

- Strong points of the paper:

The paper discusses a lot of issues that are relevant in the 
modeling of the portlet cooperation. It is good to see how they 
all affect the entire design.

 

- Any suggestions for improvements:

Restrict the number of issues discussed and concentrate on those: 
give examples of the application of those ideas on real-life cases. 
Do not refer to [13,14] all the time: by the time of the workshop 
these publications might then exist, but they hardly express previous 
work known by the readers by then.

 

5) FURTHER COMMENTS FOR AUTHORS:
 

Focus more on the real issues in the abstract (shorten it as well).

Revise the text (language): there are a number of examples 
that reveal that a native speaker could help out.

Explain in chapter 2 how different the presented work is from 
other web engineering approaches.

The User and Context Models are discussed in a way that leads 
more to questions than answers: one could consider to omit them 
altogether (or explain them better).

Give (comparative) references for all these models that are introduced!

When talking about the object-oriented models: what is wrong with OOHDM?

Explain clearly how all the viewpoints and models are combined in practice.

The "architectural style" is quite unclear.

You forgot step 3.

In 3.1 it is said "we need to": why? On the basis of what?

In the second part of 3.1 it looks like the requirements are set, 
but again they could be motivated more explicitly.

The splitting of the conceptual models neglects possible heterogeneity 
and subsequent semantical differences.

The text in 3.2 discusses both the method and the models at the same 
time and that is sometimes confusing.

The choice to use collaborations assumes that only "equalities" need 
to be expressed: is this sufficient? No calculations and transformations needed?

It would be helpful if the connection between the two viewpoints is sketched.

In the concluding remarks the innovation with respect to [3] could be improved.
