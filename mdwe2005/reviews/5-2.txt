4) JUSTIFICATION AND GENERAL COMMENTS:


  The presentation of the paper needs improvement. In general, 
it is very hard to follow the approach, partly because it is described at too high a 
level of abstraction and partly because of the bad sentence structures. For example, the
following phrase from the abstract was difficult to parse 
"...but it still not be applied as starting point in the design of conceptual schema, 
what carries out to a great effort and development time.". 
The poor presentation makes it very hard to read and judge the 
technical merits of the paper.


- Summary and main contributions:
  The paper briefly discusses an appoach to using ontologies to derive 
conceptual schemas. The existing ontology is extended to 
cover the concepts of the target application. The resulting ontology is then pruned to
exclude the parts that are not of interest.
   
- Weak points of the paper: The paper is not well-written. This makes it difficult to
read and understand.


- Strong points of the paper: The topic is relevent to the workshop


- Any suggestions for improvements: 
The paper needs to be rewritten carefully.



5) FURTHER COMMENTS FOR AUTHORS:


 - The authors include 19 references at the end of the paper, but use only 5 of them
in their paper. The authors either have to point out where the references are used, or
exclude the unsed references from the list.


 - In the introduction the authors mention "reuse" as a benifit of using ontology.
It is not clear how the reusability is increased. 


 - The related work does not have a single citation.


 - In the description of the approach, many points are described at too high a level of 
abstraction. The following are some examples:
    + It is not clear where one gets the "existing ontology" (1st paragraph in "Ontology
driven design of conceptual schema"). Also it is not clear what is meant by
"existing structure".
    + It is not clear how the ontology can be extended. Where can the information that
is needed for the extension be acquired?
    + The same comment applies to the resriction. It is not clear if the restricton can be
done automatically. As pointed out in the paper, some ontology may have up to 22,000 
concepts. Excluding them manually would be unrealistic.
