- Summary and main contributions:

The paper proposes a framework called Delta Categorisation than can be used to evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of different development approaches and methods. This approach can help developers to selected software development methods that suit particularly specific development problems.

I think that the main contribution of the paper is to provide a framework for measuring or identifying, in some way, the impact of the technological solutions on the business processes and business strategies. This proposal can be applied to categorise development methods focusing on the direction of the transformations between domains (business strategy, business operations and system implementation).

- Weak points of the paper:

The paper is too short, it should be extended by introducing more detailed examples of software development methods categorisation. Authors should develop section 4.2 by including a more detailed study of current web development methods and providing a case study where the categorisation of those methods using the Delta-Categorization approach can be well understood.

- Strong points of the paper:

It deals with an interesting issue (see the comments in the main contributions section). The paper presents an interesting framework to characterise development methods understanding the direction of the transformations between domains.


- Any suggestions for improvements:

I recommend to extend the paper (use 2 or 3 more pages) to include a more detailed study of the application of this categorisation technique to clasify current web development methods. You can use the TAS example to illustrate how a web application can influence in the business strategy of a specific organization.
